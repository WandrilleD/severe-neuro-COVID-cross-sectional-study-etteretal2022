marginalization of OLINK data on age and sex¶

The Goal of this notebook is to marginalize the effect of sex and age for each molecule on the CODEX data in order to account for differences in median age and sex between the patient groups when testing for significant differences.

2.1. linear modelling¶

Linear modelling on the log of expression (which is what NPX are) has been used in Mudd et al., Sci. Adv. 2020 on similar data.

Such a model would allow to regress out co-variables such as sex or age, which is nice.

However to be interpretable such a model rely a several important hypothesis which may or may not be respected by our dataset. Our first goal is thus to perform a brief investigation of whether or not the assumptions of the models are respected.

IMPORTANT NOTE what is shown above is by no mean a thorough and valid analysis. Rather it is a data exploration which is meant to give an intuition on the possible marginal effect of age when performing group comparison.

Conclusion when it comes to neuroCOVID stage, the model assumptions do not seem to be met with enough consistency for us to deploy this strategy and then rely on the group specific parameters to effect the testing.

Instead, we would perform a linear model, and extract the values marginalized on age and sex for each group and perform a non-parametric test on that.

NB : The effect of age is deemed real, but quite moderate.

marginalizedVals = {}

for molec in df_molecular.columns:
    results = smf.ols(molec+' ~ Age + Sex + stageGroup', data=df_merged).fit()

    ## using the model parameters to marginalize
    diffReference = sexDiff*results.params['Sex[T.m]'] + ageDiff*results.params['Age']

    marginalizedValues = df_merged[molec] - diffReference
    
    marginalizedVals[molec] = marginalizedValues

    # Creating a report of the model and marginalization as a pdf
    fig = plt.figure( figsize=(14,28) )

    gs = fig.add_gridspec(4,2)
    ax1 = fig.add_subplot(gs[0, :]) # ols report
    ax2 = fig.add_subplot(gs[1, 0]) # fitted vs residuals
    ax3 = fig.add_subplot(gs[1, 1]) # fitted vs. observed
    ax4 = fig.add_subplot(gs[2, 0]) # marginalization effect
    ax5 = fig.add_subplot(gs[2, 1]) # histogram observed, marginalization
    ax6 = fig.add_subplot(gs[3, 0]) # boxplot observed
    ax7 = fig.add_subplot(gs[3, 1]) # boxplot marginalized



    ## reporting
    ax1.text( 0,0, str( results.summary() ) , font='monospace' )
    ax1.set_title( molec )
    ax1.axis('off')


    ax2.plot( results.fittedvalues, results.resid ,'o')
    ax2.axhline(0)
    ax2.set_xlabel('fitted values')
    ax2.set_ylabel('residuals')

    ax3.plot( results.fittedvalues, results.resid+results.fittedvalues ,'o')
    m = min(results.fittedvalues)
    M = max(results.fittedvalues)
    ax3.plot( [m,M] , [m,M] , color='red')
    ax3.set_xlabel('fitted values')
    ax3.set_ylabel('observed values')


    ax4.hist(diffReference)
    ax4.set_xlabel("marginalization effect")

    ax5.set_xlabel( "marginalized values" )
